Lúba Neamhnithe · Interregnum Nullificans
The Nullification Loop — five stages, zero determinations

Every failure traced in this record has the same root cause. The State declined to recognise what was placed before it — not to evaluate, not to reject, not to engage on its merits — simply not to see it. Once you understand how non-recognition works as a procedural technique, the pattern becomes legible across every department, every committee, every parliamentary channel, every EU obligation.

Stage 01
Acknowledgement as Closure
Receipt confirmed. No pathway follows. Files sometimes deleted.
Stage 02
Remit Displacement
Passed laterally. No body holds the full objective. Jurisdictional evaporation.
Stage 03
Deferral without Destination
Action postponed. No timetable. No accountable actor.
Stage 04
Sufficiency without Audit
Existing arrangements asserted as adequate. No evidence furnished.
Stage 05
Record Suppression
Institutional memory does not persist. The loop resets. The matter resurfaces unchanged.
The diagnostic frame · three non-exclusive hypotheses
α
Hypothesis Alpha
Structural Non-Governance
The Irish State lacks the institutional architecture to process O.R. submissions of this character. The failure is design-failure, not agent-failure. The system produces ignoring as its default output.
β
Hypothesis Beta
Misfeasance in Public Office
Individual officeholders, once formally notified, are subject to the misfeasance threshold. Knowledge + inaction + harm = tort. Several named officeholders have reached this threshold.
γ
Hypothesis Gamma
Epistemic Failure
The State cannot evaluate what it cannot categorise. O.R. as methodology falls outside existing evaluation grammar. Correction requires both a writ and a curriculum change.
§ 01 — What the law says about this

The tort chain —
joint and several liability

Plain-language guide to what remedies exist and who bears responsibility. From the FLAC strategic litigation brief.

Definition · tort of conversion

Conversion is the wrongful taking, retention, or disposal of another person's property. If you hand your materials to an institution for evaluation, and that institution keeps them for nineteen years without evaluating them, returning them, compensating you, or obtaining a licence to hold them — that is conversion. The Civil Liability Act 1961 makes multiple wrongdoers jointly and severally liable: each is fully responsible for the whole harm, not just their individual share.

I
Mandamus — compel the State to do what it was always required to do

The court can issue an order of mandamus requiring the competent authority to discharge its function under EU Regulation 2021/821 — to actually evaluate the submitted corpus and produce a determination. This is not punitive. It is the most basic of remedies: an instruction to do the job required nineteen years ago.

Order 84 RSC · mandamus · competent authority function · Regulation 2021/821 Art. 2(1)
II
Certiorari — quash the two decisions that tried to close the door

Two specific decisions exist within the reviewable twelve-month window. The McEntee Foreclosure (February 4th, 2026): the Department of Defence closed the file using an authority that holds no function under Regulation 2021/821. The Gunn Letter (February 24th, 2026): the new Clerk of Dáil Éireann ratified a determination she had not made or reviewed, offered no procedural pathway, and failed to address constitutional objections on the record. Certiorari quashes both.

McEntee Foreclosure DOD-MO-01201-2025 · Gunn Letter Feb 24 2026 · O'Keeffe v An Bord Pleanála [1993] · Re Haughey [1971]
III
Declaratory relief — establish what the State is constitutionally required to do

Declarations that bind the State permanently: that the competent authority function must be operationally discharged; that ministerial discretion cannot be indefinitely suspended in the face of a statutory obligation; that the structural foreclosure of every domestic review channel makes access to justice illusory under Article 47 of the EU Charter. Once declared, these bind the State for every future citizen who submits material to a public body under a statutory framework.

Art. 47 CFR · effective remedy · Art. 40.3.1 Bunreacht · Art. 228 TFEU · EU principle of effectiveness
IV
Damages — compensation for what was taken and what was withheld

Multiple heads of damage. Conversion: nineteen years of retention of 26 kilograms of proprietary O.R. materials without evaluation, compensation, or lawful basis. Loss of opportunity: the compounding opportunity cost of non-deployment of a turnkey O.R. corpus across nineteen years. Personal consequence: documented measurable impact on the applicant. The tortfeasor chain is joint and several under the Civil Liability Act 1961.

Civil Liability Act 1961 · joint and several liability · tort of conversion · opportunity cost · FLAC Strategic Litigation Brief 2026
V
Protective Costs Order — so that money cannot be used to silence a public interest claim

An application for a Protective Costs Order at the leave stage. If the case is lost, the applicant is not required to pay the State's legal costs — otherwise the threat of those costs would deter any citizen from challenging a nineteen-year governance failure. The EU principle of effectiveness provides an independent basis: enforcement of rights derived from EU law must not be excessively difficult or costly.

Dunne v Minister for the Environment [2007] IESC 60 · EU principle of effectiveness · Art. 47 CFR

§ 03 — The European dimension

The matter has now reached
every level of the EU architecture

On 27 May 2026, the Council Secretariat was formally saisi. The clock is running at EU level regardless of domestic resolution.

EU Regulation 2021/821 · what it requires · what Ireland failed to do

Regulation 2021/821 is the EU's primary instrument governing the control of exports, transfer, brokering, and transit of dual-use items. Article 2(1) requires each Member State to designate a competent authority and ensure it functions. Ireland's designated CA (Dept of Foreign Affairs, 2009–2024) never conducted a single technical evaluation of the submitted O.R. corpus across eighteen years and four transposition cycles. The designation was then transferred to DETE in August 2024 without audit of the accumulated obligation. The Commission's mandatory evaluation opens no later than September 10th, 2026.

Formal Saisine — Thérèse Blanchet

May 27th, 2026. The General Secretariat of the Council of the EU was formally saisi of Ireland's competent authority vacancy under Reg. 2021/821. Once saisi, the Secretariat is obligated to record the matter and ensure the relevant Council formations are on notice before the Presidency opens.

The Presidency Paradox · structural, not rhetorical

"The Member State chairing the Council workstreams governing Regulation 2021/821 during its mandatory evaluation is the Member State whose competent authority architecture is the most structurally deficient in the Union."

Ireland assumes the COMPET chair on July 1st, 2026. The Commission's evaluation of Regulation 2021/821 opens on or before September 10th, 2026 — seventy-one days into the Irish Presidency. Ireland will chair the Council workstreams on that instrument at precisely the moment when its own eighteen-year non-performance under it is assessed. Domestic resolution before July 1st is the only outcome that prevents this from becoming a permanent feature of Ireland's compliance record.

§ 04 — The chronological record

Not a grievance.
A directed instrument.

Each entry is dated and independently reviewable. The record now extends to the EU institutional level.

Nov 2006

First formal submission · 26 kilograms of O.R. materials enter State custody

The corpus is formally presented to Irish authorities. Acknowledged. Not evaluated. Not returned. Not licensed. The Department of Foreign Affairs holds the CA designation from 2009 — eighteen years of non-performance begin here.

Feb–Sep 2009

Minister Martin · 4.5-hour technical presentation · verbal commitments made and broken

In February, a 4.5-hour technical presentation through Minister Martin's office. Both his Deputy Director and his private secretary confirmed a ministerial report would follow. On September 25th, Martin gave a further verbal commitment in person. The promised report was never produced. The embargo held for fourteen years. He is now Taoiseach.

2006–2024

Six governments. Seven Oireachtas committees. Zero determinations.

Every parliamentary channel available to a citizen operating in good faith was engaged. The JCPP received eighteen formal communications and produced zero outputs. The JCEUA deleted formally received correspondence. Both Clerks issued simultaneous Section 8(3) determinations applying a standard absent from the statute. The Loop ran on every contact.

Aug 2024

CA designation transferred to DETE · without audit of accumulated obligation

The Control of Exports Act 2023 commenced, transferring the CA designation from Foreign Affairs to DETE. The transfer occurred without audit of eighteen years of non-performance, without transmission of institutional memory, without any mechanism requiring the receiving department to assess unprocessed material. The accumulated obligation was structurally abandoned at the moment of handover.

Feb 2025

Storm Éowyn · O.R. corpus in State custody, unused, since 2006

Ireland's emergency response to Storm Éowyn exposed coordination failures that O.R. methodology is specifically designed to prevent. The OEP, in its June 2025 adequacy conclusion, stated it was "swamped" with severe weather response. The technology designed to prevent precisely that class of failure had been in State custody, unprocessed, for eighteen years at that point.

Feb 2026

Two reviewable determinations · McEntee Foreclosure · Gunn Letter

February 4th: the Department of Defence closed the file using the Office of Emergency Planning — a body with no function under Regulation 2021/821. February 24th: the new Clerk of Dáil Éireann ratified a determination she had not reviewed, offered no pathway, and ignored constitutional objections on the record. Both acts are independently reviewable within the twelve-month window.

May 2026

Council Secretariat formally saisi · COMPET handover notice · Séjourné notified

May 27th, 2026: three simultaneous transmissions. The General Secretariat (Secretary General Blanchet) is formally saisi of the CA vacancy. The outgoing COMPET Chair (Cyprus) is placed on formal notice before the handover. EVP Séjourné's cabinet is notified. MEP Dolors Montserrat (PETI President), Minister Thomas Byrne (incoming GAC Chair), and Minister Peter Burke (incoming COMPET Chair) are all copied. The matter is now visible at every level of the EU institutional architecture simultaneously.

1 Jul 2026

Irish Presidency opens · the Presidency Paradox becomes live

Ireland assumes the rotating Presidency of the Council of the European Union and takes the chair of COMPET — the configuration that administers Regulation 2021/821 — while holding an eighteen-year CA vacancy on that instrument. Thirty-five days into the Presidency, the Commission's mandatory evaluation window opens (September 10th, 2026).

10 Sep 2026

Commission evaluation of Reg. 2021/821 opens · Ireland's compliance record enters the institutional record

The Commission's mandatory evaluation opens no later than September 10th, 2026. Ireland's competent authority architecture — fragmented across three departments with no statutory coordination mechanism, and carrying eighteen years of zero-determination — enters that evaluation as an environmental fact. The Council Secretariat's saisine of May 27th, 2026 is already in the institutional file.
