# **START HERE**

## ***Governmentality-2025: A Public Record of Administrative Failure and Civic Remedy***

---

## **What This Is**

This project documents a single, verifiable claim:

**That the Irish State repeatedly received expert, actionable intelligence on systemic governance risk — and failed to absorb or act on it.**

Over time, this failure moved through four phases:

1. **Expert Warning**  
2. **Public Explanation**  
3. **Media Disclosure**  
4. **Legal Indictment**

Each phase is preserved here, in public, as a matter of record.

---

## **What This Is *Not***

* Not a political campaign  
* Not a partisan critique  
* Not a theory or speculation  
* Not a retrospective opinion

Everything here is **documented, timestamped, and attributable**.

---

## **How to Navigate This Record**

The material is organised by **audience and function**, not by chronology alone. You do not need to read everything.

### **Choose your entry point:**

* **Citizens & general readers** → *Public Relations*  
* **Journalists & researchers** → *Press*  
* **Experts & policymakers** → *Governmentality-2025*  
* **Lawyers, judges, ethics bodies** → *Legal & Statutory Repository*

All four describe the *same failure* — from different vantage points.

## **1\. Governmentality-2025**

### ***The Technical and Institutional Core***

This is the **analytical backbone** of the project.

It documents:

* the **science of governance** (Operations Research, cybernetics, decision theory),  
* the **availability of that science** to the State,  
* and the structural reasons it was not adopted.

**Key question answered here:**

*Was the State technically capable of understanding and acting on these signals?*

**Answer:** Yes.

This repository establishes that the failure was **institutional**, not intellectual.

[Access](https://github.com/dr-wojak/governmentality-2025)

## **2\. Public Relations**

### ***The Civic Interface***

Public Relations materials exist to answer a different question:

**Was this information made accessible to the public in good faith?**

These documents:

* translate expert intelligence into plain language,  
* explain risks without jargon,  
* and remove any claim that the issue was “too technical” to grasp.

This section exists to defeat the **obscurity defence**.

If the State claims it did not understand, these materials show:

* the explanation was already provided,  
* clearly,  
* publicly,  
* and repeatedly.

[Access](https://github.com/dr-wojak/public-relations)

## **4\. Legal & Statutory Repository**

### ***The Jurisprudential Record***

The Legal Repository marks the formal transition from civic disclosure to **statutory accountability**.

It functions as a **body of evidence**, containing:

* ethics complaints,  
* affidavits,  
* constitutional petitions,  
* jurisprudential briefs,  
* and procedural correspondence.

**Key question answered here:**

*At what point does ignored expertise become a breach of duty?*

This repository asserts — with legal instruments — that:

* applied intelligence forms part of the **Duty of Care**,  
* administrative silence can constitute **fiduciary breach**,  
* and failure to reason is justiciable.

[Access](https://github.com/dr-wojak/legal)

## **Why This Is Public**

Because democratic governance depends on **symmetry of information**.

Once the signal is documented:

* ignorance is no longer plausible,  
* delay becomes visible,  
* and silence acquires meaning.

This project exists so that future inquiries, courts, journalists, and citizens do not have to reconstruct the failure from memory or hearsay.

The record is already assembled.
