§ 01 — The root cause

Non-recognition as
a procedural technique

Once you understand what it is, you can see it operating across every institution that has touched this matter.

What was submitted

Twenty-six kilograms of Operations Research methodology

From November 2006, a corpus of O.R. materials — the mathematical science behind emergency logistics, resource allocation, crisis management — was formally presented to Irish authorities. Twenty-six kilograms of proprietary research. Formally received. Never evaluated. Never returned. Never licensed. Still in State custody. That is the foundation of the tort of conversion.

First presented: Nov 2006 · Dept of Foreign Affairs CA designation held 2009–2024 · Now DETE · Zero determinations across four transposition cycles
What non-recognition does

The technique that makes evaluation permanently unnecessary

Non-recognition is not a decision to reject. It is a decision to not-see. A submission that is not recognised need not be evaluated, and without evaluation there is no determination, and without determination there is no reviewable act, and without a reviewable act there is no basis for judicial challenge. Every stage of the Nullification Loop follows logically from the first move: decline to formally see what is placed before you.

Nullification Loop · five moves · acknowledgement without recognition · no closing condition
Why it matters to everyone

The damage is not personal — it is to the entirety of the citizenry

O.R. methodology improves how every emergency is managed, how every scarce resource is allocated, how every crisis is coordinated. When the State declines to recognise a life-preserving methodology, it does not merely wrong the researcher. It withholds that capability from every citizen who would have benefited from better-planned emergency responses, better-allocated public resources, better-coordinated crisis management. Storm Éowyn is one named example.

Storm Éowyn · Feb 2025 · O.R. corpus in State custody, unprocessed, since 2006 · avoidable systemic risk
The Nullification Loop · the same mechanism, every time

"Acknowledge, defer, displace, assert sufficiency, reset — the same output regardless of who operates the mechanism, regardless of which government is in office, regardless of how many channels are engaged."

The Loop has five moves. Each move produces the same output: no determination. The first move is non-recognition. Once you see that move, you see it at every stage: the acknowledgement that closes instead of opens; the referral that names no body; the deferral that specifies no date; the sufficiency assertion backed by no audit; the reset that leaves no trace for the next administration. Every failure in this record is a variation on the same technique.

§ 02 — Misfeasance and malfeasance

When "no good action"
becomes deliberate harm

The distinction matters. It defines what kind of wrong this is, and what remedies are available.

Plain-language definition · misfeasance in public office

Misfeasance in public office occurs when a public officer — a minister, a clerk, a civil servant — fails to discharge a duty they hold by virtue of their office, and that failure causes harm. It does not require bad faith. A minister who receives a statutory submission and does nothing with it — not out of malice but out of comfort, convenience, or indifference — has committed misfeasance. The harm to the public is the same regardless of motive.

Misfeasance

Nineteen years of no good action

Every government from 2006 to 2026 received this material. Not one produced a determination. Not a yes, not a no, not a reasoned refusal, not a statement of inapplicability. Six governments. Six opportunities to discharge a statutory duty. Zero discharges. That is the very definition of misfeasance: the failure to perform a public duty, producing harm to those who depended on its performance.

Six governments · Art. 40.3.1 Bunreacht · Art. 28.4.1 collective responsibility · positive duty to vindicate personal rights
Malfeasance in prospect

When the corpus becomes a state secret

Misfeasance can transmute into malfeasance — active wrongdoing — when the motives for non-recognition become institutionalised. When a corpus of proprietary research is converted into what functions as a state secret by the very hands that absorbed it without licence, evaluation, compensation, or return, the question of motive becomes unavoidable. At that point, the failure is no longer merely passive. It is the exercise of institutional power to conceal what was received.

Tort of conversion · proprietary O.R. materials held without licence · without evaluation · without return · since 2006
The three hypotheses

Architectural, deliberate, or negligent — on every reading the duty was not discharged

The Tairiscint Mheinhardt identifies three possible characterisations of the breach: architectural dysfunction (the system has no closing condition by design), misfeasance (public officers failed to perform duties they held), or negligence so severe it constitutes its own category of failure. On every reading, the harm is established. On every reading, the remedy is available. The variable is only motive — and motive defines the scale of liability.

Tairiscint Mheinhardt v17 · three hypotheses · stakeholder liability · defined by motive
Collective responsibility · Art. 28.4.1

"Six departments simultaneously acknowledging a statutory submission and producing no determination is collective executive responsibility performed rather than discharged. The distinction is constitutional, not rhetorical."

The Irish Constitution requires the Government to be collectively responsible to the Dáil for all executive action. Collective responsibility requires collective reason. A submission that passes through six departments and produces no reasoned output from any of them is not a sequence of individual oversights. It is a collective constitutional failure — and that failure is now documented, publicly accessible, and anchored to a live regulatory deadline.

§ 03 — What the law says about this

The tort chain —
joint and several liability

Plain-language guide to what remedies exist and who bears responsibility. From the FLAC strategic litigation brief.

Definition · tort of conversion (plain language)

Conversion is the wrongful taking, retention, or disposal of another person's property. If you hand your materials to an institution for evaluation, and that institution keeps them for nineteen years without evaluating them, returning them, compensating you, or obtaining a licence to hold them — that is conversion. The Civil Liability Act 1961 makes multiple wrongdoers jointly and severally liable: each is fully responsible for the whole harm, not just their individual share.

I
Mandamus — compel the State to do what it was always required to do

The court can issue an order of mandamus requiring the competent authority to discharge its function under EU Regulation 2021/821 — to actually evaluate the submitted corpus and produce a determination. This is not punitive. It is the most basic of remedies: an instruction to do the job that was required nineteen years ago.

Order 84 RSC · mandamus · competent authority function · Regulation 2021/821 Art. 2(1)
II
Certiorari — quash the two decisions that tried to close the door

Two specific decisions exist within the reviewable twelve-month window. The McEntee Foreclosure (February 4th, 2026): the Department of Defence closed the file using an authority — the Office of Emergency Planning — that holds no function under Regulation 2021/821. That is action without proper authority, reviewable on four independent irrationality grounds. The Gunn Letter (February 24th, 2026): the new Clerk of Dáil Éireann ratified a determination she had not made and had not reviewed, offered no procedural pathway, and failed to address constitutional objections on the record. Certiorari quashes both.

McEntee Foreclosure DOD-MO-01201-2025 · Gunn Letter Feb 24 2026 · O'Keeffe v An Bord Pleanála [1993] 1 IR 39 · Re Haughey [1971] IR 217
III
Declaratory relief — establish what the State is constitutionally required to do

Declarations that bind the State permanently: that the competent authority function must be operationally discharged, not merely formally designated; that ministerial discretion cannot be indefinitely suspended in the face of a statutory obligation; that the structural foreclosure of every domestic review channel makes access to justice illusory under Article 47 of the EU Charter. Once declared, these bind the State for every future citizen who submits material to a public body under a statutory framework.

Art. 47 CFR · effective remedy · Art. 40.3.1 Bunreacht · Art. 228 TFEU · EU principle of effectiveness
IV
Damages — compensation for what was taken and what was withheld

Multiple heads of damage. Conversion: nineteen years of retention of 26 kilograms of proprietary O.R. materials without evaluation, compensation, or lawful basis. Loss of opportunity: the compounding opportunity cost of non-deployment of a turnkey O.R. corpus across nineteen years — benchmarked against actual public expenditure decisions that did not use it. Personal consequence: documented measurable impact on the applicant across the period. The tortfeasor chain is joint and several under the Civil Liability Act 1961.

Civil Liability Act 1961 · joint and several liability · tort of conversion · opportunity cost · FLAC Strategic Litigation Brief 2026
V
Protective Costs Order — so that money cannot be used to silence a public interest claim

An application for a Protective Costs Order will be made at the leave stage. This means that if the case is lost, the applicant is not required to pay the State's legal costs — otherwise the threat of those costs would deter any citizen from challenging a nineteen-year governance failure. The EU principle of effectiveness provides an independent basis: enforcement of rights derived from EU law must not be made excessively difficult or costly.

Dunne v Minister for the Environment [2007] IESC 60 · EU principle of effectiveness · Art. 47 CFR · public interest litigation

§ 05 — The European dimension

The matter has now reached
every level of the EU architecture

On 27 May 2026, the Council Secretariat was formally saisi. The clock is running at EU level regardless of domestic resolution.

EU Regulation 2021/821 · what it requires · what Ireland failed to do

Regulation 2021/821 is the EU's primary instrument governing the control of exports, transfer, brokering, and transit of dual-use items — technologies with both civilian and military applications. Article 2(1) requires each Member State to designate a competent authority and ensure it functions. Ireland's designated CA (Dept of Foreign Affairs, 2009–2024) never conducted a single technical evaluation of the submitted O.R. corpus across eighteen years and four transposition cycles. The designation was then transferred to DETE in August 2024 without audit of the accumulated obligation — so the new CA inherited the function without institutional memory of what it had not done. The Commission's mandatory evaluation of the Regulation opens no later than September 10th, 2026.

The Presidency Paradox · structural, not rhetorical

"The Member State chairing the Council workstreams governing Regulation 2021/821 during its mandatory evaluation is the Member State whose competent authority architecture is the most structurally deficient in the Union."

Ireland assumes the COMPET chair on July 1st, 2026. The Commission's evaluation of Regulation 2021/821 opens on or before September 10th, 2026 — seventy-one days into the Irish Presidency. Ireland will chair the Council workstreams on that instrument at precisely the moment when its own eighteen-year non-performance under it is assessed. This is not a rhetorical point. § 07 — What is being asked

Three asks.
Each proportionate, each grounded.

Not vindication. Accountability, mechanism, and public comprehension — on evidence that is complete and publicly accessible.

Legal

A reasoned determination — with reasons, within a reasonable time

A formal appraisal of the submitted corpus by the designated competent authority, with stated reasons. Article 41 of the EU Charter makes this an enforceable right. Order 84 RSC provides the domestic mechanism. Nineteen years is not a reasonable time. The two reviewable determinations provide the immediate legal entry point.

Art. 41 CFR · Order 84 RSC · mandamus · certiorari · McEntee + Gunn reviewable acts
Structural

A statutory appraisal mechanism — so this cannot happen to the next citizen

The Administrative Determination (Operations Research and Technology Submissions) Bill 2026 provides the statutory architecture. Without a closing condition — a mechanism that requires a determination — the Loop resets with every change of government and every future citizen who submits material of strategic public value faces the same nineteen-year non-answer. The structural fix is already drafted.

Administrative Determination Bill 2026 · submitted to Seanad · closing condition · statutory mechanism
Civic

Public comprehension — so it can be named, recognised, and not repeated

The pattern documented here is not unique to this corpus. Non-recognition as a procedural technique can be applied to any citizen's submission on any matter of strategic public value. Once the public understands what it looks like, they can identify it when they see it. That is the civic purpose of publishing this record.
